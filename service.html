Services

Biodiversity conservation

Biodiversity conservation is the protection, management, and restoration of biological diversity, which includes all species of plants, animals, and microorganisms, as well as the ecosystems and habitats in which they live. Biodiversity is essential for the functioning of ecosystems and the provision of ecosystem services that are important for human well-being, including air and water purification, soil health, and climate regulation.

Ecosystem services

Ecosystem services are the benefits that humans receive from the natural environment and its ecosystems. These services are critical for our well-being and are the foundation of our economies and societies. Provisioning services are the tangible products that we obtain from ecosystems, such as food, water, timber, and fuel. Regulating services are the services that help to regulate the environment, such as climate regulation, water purification, and pest control. Supporting services are the services that ecosystems provide to maintain the conditions for life, such as nutrient cycling, soil formation, and biodiversity. Finally, cultural services are the non-material benefits that we receive from ecosystems, such as recreation, aesthetic enjoyment, and spiritual and cultural values.

Species conservation

Species conservation is the protection and management of individual species, including threatened and endangered species, to prevent their extinction and promote their recovery. Species conservation is critical for maintaining biodiversity and ecosystem functioning, as well as for the cultural and aesthetic value of individual species. Species conservation involves a range of activities, including habitat management and restoration, captive breeding and reintroduction, invasive species control, and public education and awareness. The ultimate goal of species conservation is to prevent the extinction of threatened and endangered species and to promote their recovery to a self-sustaining population size.

Habitat Management

Habitat management is a key service in wildlife conservation that involves the restoration, preservation, and enhancement of habitats for wildlife populations. Wildlife habitats are critical for the survival and thriving of different species as they provide food, shelter, breeding sites, and migration routes. Habitat management practices aim to create or maintain a healthy and sustainable ecosystem that supports wildlife populations and their ecological functions. Habitat management practices may include reforestation, restoration of wetlands, removal of invasive species, controlled burns to prevent wildfires, and creation of wildlife corridors. Reforestation involves planting native tree species in areas where forests have been degraded or cleared. Restoration of wetlands involves the creation or restoration of wetland ecosystems to support waterfowl and other wetland-dependent species.

PM Modi delighted over release of 2 cheetahs in Kuno National Park's bigger enclosure

All the eight cheetahs, who have been brought from Namibia to India, are healthy , active and adjusting well, prime minister Narendra Modi said today. He also expressed his delight over the release of two cheetahs in the bigger enclosure, who were reintroduced in India in Madhya Pradesh's Kuno National Park in September, after completing their mandatory quarantine. The Prime Minister said that the other cheetahs will be released soon.

India to launch project to conserve Gangetic dolphins

In September 2021, India's Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change announced plans to launch a project to conserve the endangered Gangetic river dolphins. The project will involve habitat restoration, pollution control measures, and community engagement to protect this iconic species.

Court orders ban on elephant rides at Amer Fort:

In October 2021, the Rajasthan High Court ordered a ban on elephant rides at the Amer Fort in Jaipur, citing concerns about animal welfare and the negative impact on elephant populations. This is seen as a positive step towards promoting responsible tourism and protecting elephants from exploitation.
